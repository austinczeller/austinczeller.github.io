Interests

Species Habitat Use

Understanding how different species utilize their habitats and the factors that influence distributions are fundamental questions in ecology and have heightened importance during the Anthropocene.

Quantitative Ecology

I have a strong interest in applying quantitative methods to address ecological questions and develop evidence-based management strategies.

Land Management

In a finite world, sustainable land management practices are essential for preserving biodiversity and ecosystem services.

Indigenous Stewardship

Reconceptualizing conservation through the lens of Indigenous knowledge systems and traditional ecological practices is essential for reconciliation and sustainable management.

Current projects

Now
Cumulative effects influence northern boreal breeding bird abundances in a mining impacted landscape
With M. Brown, D. Yip, E. M. Bayne, A. McCulley, M. Hornseth, C. L. Mahon, C. Mantyka-Pringle

We are evaluating the various effects mining, particularly placer mining, has on various species of birds in central Yukon, Canada. In doing so we have determined individual relationships between species and features on the landscape, as well as identifying critical thresholds of disturbance in which species are most affected. Furthermore, we identify habitat types that are more vulnerable or resilient to disturbance.

Now
Improving habitat models for Pileated Woodpeckers: The roles of occupancy, intensity of use, land cover, and scale
With A. De Rosa, L. Leston, C. L. Mahon, E. M. Bayne

We are determining the best models for predicting Pileated Woodpecker habitat use in Alberta, Canada. In doing so we compare how occupancy and intensity of use models can accurately predict habitat use, and how the inclusion of various land cover data and scale can improve model performance.
