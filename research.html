Indigenous Stewardship

Current projects

Now
Cumulative effects influence northern boreal breeding bird abundances in a mining impacted landscape
With M. Brown, D. Yip, E.M. Bayne, A.McCulley, M. Hornseth, C.L. Mahon, C. Mantyka-Pringle
We are evaluating the various effects mining, particularly placer mining, has on various speices of birds in central Yukon, Canada. In doing so we have determined indvidual relationships between species and features on the landscape, as well as identifying critical thresholds of disturbance in which species are most affected by disturbance. Furthermore, we idenitfy habitat types that are more vulnerable/resilient to disturbance.
Now
Improving habitat models for Pileated Woodpeckers: The roles of occupancy, intensity of use, land cover, and scale
With A. De Rosa, L. Leston, C.L. Mahon, E.M. Bayne
We are determining the best models for predicting Pileated Woodpecker habitat use in Alberta, Canada. In doing so we compare how occupancy and intensity of use models can accurately predict habitat use, and how the inclusion of various land cover data and scale can improve model performance.
